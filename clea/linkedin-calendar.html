Tuesday Education
The 5-part design audit every pre-seed startup should run before spending on ads
Practical framework post — high save/share potential
📋 Listicle / Framework — carousel or text
Before you spend a single rupee (or dollar) on paid ads — Run this 5-part design audit on your site: 1. The 5-second test Can a stranger tell what you do, who it's for, and what to do next — in 5 seconds? If not, your hero section needs a rewrite. Not more traffic. 2. CTA visibility Is your primary CTA above the fold on mobile? Is it one clear action — not three? "Get started", "Book a call", and "Learn more" on the same screen = decision paralysis. 3. Visual hierarchy check Where does the eye land first? Does it guide toward conversion or toward your nav bar? 4. Trust signal audit Social proof at the point of decision — not buried at the bottom. Logos, outcomes, testimonials close to CTAs. 5. Page speed reality check If your LCP is above 3 seconds, you're losing 30%+ of visitors before they read a single word. Design problems masquerade as traffic problems all the time. Fix the design. Then buy the ads. Save this for your next site review.

Tuesday Education
The 6 schema markup types every SaaS startup website needs (and most miss)
Tactical SEO content — extremely saveable for founders and dev teams
📋 Listicle — high save potential
If your SaaS site doesn't have structured data, AI search engines can't easily cite you. Here are the 6 schema types your startup website needs in 2025: 1. Organization schema Your company name, URL, logo, social profiles. The baseline. Missing on ~60% of startup sites. 2. SoftwareApplication schema Tells AI what your product does, its category, price range, and platform. Critical for AEO. 3. FAQPage schema Wraps your FAQ content in a format AI search can directly quote. Each Q&A becomes a citable answer. 4. HowTo schema Perfect for tutorial content and onboarding guides. Google and AI surfaces love step-by-step structure. 5. Review / AggregateRating schema Pull in your G2, Capterra, or Trustpilot ratings as rich signals. Massive trust boost in AI summaries. 6. BreadcrumbList schema Helps AI understand your site structure and content hierarchy. Often ignored. Rarely optional. Adding these takes a few hours of dev time or a plugin install. The compounding SEO and AEO benefit lasts for years. Want a free schema audit for your site? Drop your URL in the comments.

Tuesday Education
The homepage hierarchy formula we use for every startup client (steal this)
Tactical framework — extremely high save rate
📋 Framework post — carousel-ready
We've audited 40+ startup homepages this year. Here's the section order that converts best. Steal it: Section 1: Hero Outcome headline + 1-line sub (who it's for) + single CTA + social proof number (users, customers, or rating) Section 2: Problem Agitation 3 bullets. The pain your ICP feels right now. Written in their words — not your product language. Section 3: Solution Overview How you solve it. Feature names are okay here — but always paired with the outcome. Section 4: Social Proof 3 testimonials with: Name + Title + Company + one measurable outcome. Not "Great product!" quotes. Section 5: How It Works 3–4 step visual. Reduce the perceived effort of getting started. Section 6: Objection Handling FAQ section answering the top 3 reasons someone wouldn't buy. Schema-marked up. Section 7: Final CTA Repeat the primary CTA. Add urgency or incentive if you have one. That's it. 7 sections. Proven order. Save this. Use it in your next site refresh. What section is most broken on your current homepage?

Why your startup's mobile experience is silently destroying your SEO — and 4 fixes
Practical SEO/design crossover — relevant to every startup with a website
📋 Problem + fixes format
Google uses your mobile site to rank your desktop site. Not the other way around. Mobile-first indexing has been default since 2020. But most startup sites I audit are still built desktop-first — with mobile as an afterthought. Here's what that costs you in SEO: → Images not compressed for mobile = LCP above 3s = ranking penalty → Text too small for mobile tap targets = high bounce = lower engagement signals → Nav menus that break on small screens = Googlebot can't crawl your pages properly → CTAs hidden below the fold on mobile = conversions drop 60%+ 4 fixes that take less than a week: 1. Run your site through Google's PageSpeed Insights on mobile — not desktop. Fix the red items first. 2. Audit your tap targets. Every button should be at least 44x44px. Zoom in on your phone and tap around. 3. Check your font size — minimum 16px body text on mobile. Anything smaller and Google flags it. 4. Test your CTA placement on iPhone 12 screen size specifically. Is your primary action visible without scrolling? Design for the phone in your customer's hand. Rank accordingly. What's your current mobile PageSpeed score?

How to build a 90-day growth roadmap for your startup website in 3 hours
Flagship how-to — the most-saved post of the month
📋 Step-by-step guide — carousel or long-form text
You don't need a full agency to get clarity on your growth priorities. Here's how to build your own 90-day website roadmap in 3 hours: Hour 1: Audit (60 min) Open Google Search Console. Find your top 10 landing pages by impressions. For each: What's the click-through rate? What's the conversion rate? Most founders discover 1–2 high-impression pages with terrible CTR — that's your quick win. Hour 2: Prioritise (60 min) Sort findings into 3 buckets: • Week 1–2: Copy changes, CTA placement, above-fold fixes • Month 2: Schema markup, blog interlinking, mobile speed • Month 3: New landing pages, programmatic content, case studies Prioritise by: impact × effort. High impact, low effort goes first. Always. Hour 3: Document + Assign (60 min) One Google Doc. One table. Each row: Problem / Fix / Owner / Timeline / Success Metric. Share it with your dev and content team. Book a weekly 15-min check-in. That's it. No agency needed to get started.
