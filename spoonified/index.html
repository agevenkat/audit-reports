Executive Summary
Core problem: Spoonified has a genuinely novel product — an AI-powered utensil that passively tracks nutrition and eating behavior. But the site is invisible to search engines, absent from AI-generated answers, and has no content, no schema, and no lead nurture infrastructure. Every page shares the same title tag. The blog is live but empty. The waitlist form captures leads that go cold. The site's SEO health score of 28/100 reflects a digital presence that exists in name only.

Critical All 4 pages share the title tag "World's First Intelligent Utensil" — brand name absent, zero keyword signal On-Page SEO ▾
Problem

spoonified.me, /about, /insights, and /contact-us all render the same <title>: "World's First Intelligent Utensil". The brand name "Spoonified" does not appear in any title tag. The phrase "World's First Intelligent Utensil" has near-zero search volume and is not differentiated by page.

Opportunity

Unique, keyword-rich title tags are the single highest-leverage on-page SEO element. Fixing all 4 titles moves every page from effectively unranked to eligible for its target query. The homepage can begin competing for "AI nutrition tracker" (~8K/mo) and "smart spoon" (~2K/mo) within 30 days of indexation.

How to Fix

In Framer: Page Settings → SEO tab → set unique title per page.

Homepage: "Spoonified — AI Smart Spoon for Automatic Nutrition Tracking"
About: "About Spoonified | Intelligent Utensil for Mindful Eating"
Insights: "Spoonified Insights | Smart Eating & Nutrition Tech Blog"
Contact: "Join the Spoonified Alpha Waitlist | AI-Powered Utensil"

Expected Result

All 4 pages re-indexed with proper titles within 2–3 weeks of sitemap resubmission. CTR from branded SERP appearances estimated to improve 30–50%. Homepage becomes eligible to rank for "smart nutrition tracker" and related terms.

Critical No meta descriptions on any page — Google auto-generates low-CTR snippets from duplicated DOM text On-Page SEO ▾
Problem

Zero <meta name="description"> tags are set on any of the 4 live pages. Framer renders each content block 3× in the DOM (desktop/tablet/mobile variants). Keep all under 158 characters.

Expected Result

Google picks up new descriptions within 1–2 weeks of crawling (accelerate via GSC URL inspection). Branded SERP appearance becomes recognizable and clickable. Contributes to improved overall CTR baseline before any content is published.

Branded CTR ↑ 15–25% in 2 weeks
Critical Framer renders all content 3× in the DOM — crawlers see repeated body text as thin/duplicate content Technical SEO ▾
Problem

Every text block on the homepage appears exactly 3 times in the fetched HTML — e.g., "Transforming bites into insights for a healthier tomorrow" renders once for mobile, tablet, and desktop variants simultaneously. The same is true for all feature copy and section headers. Googlebot sees triplicated keyword signals and low unique content ratio.

Opportunity

Fixing this immediately improves keyword density accuracy, eliminates potential "keyword stuffing" signals, and reduces page weight. For a site with only 4 pages, content uniqueness is critical — every instance of triplication dilutes the effective content-to-markup ratio that search engines use to evaluate page quality.

How to Fix

In Framer: for each text/section component, check the Breakpoints panel. Use Framer's native Visibility toggles per breakpoint instead of duplicating the component. If duplication is intentional for layout, add aria-hidden="true" and display:none to the non-primary variants so Googlebot ignores them. Validate with Google's Rich Results Test after fix.

Expected Result

Correct keyword density signals within 1–2 crawl cycles (~30 days). Page weight reduction of ~30% improves LCP score. Reduces risk of algorithmic thin-content penalty as the site scales to more pages.

Page quality signals improved in 30 days

Problem

How to Fix

Add via Framer: Page Settings → Custom Code → <head> → paste JSON-LD blocks.

Priority schemas:

2. Product on homepage (name, brand, description, offers: {availability})
3. FAQPage on a new /faq page (15–20 Q&As)
4. Person for each team member on /about

Expected Result

Organization and Product schema indexed within 2–3 weeks. FAQPage schema eligible for Google AI Overviews and Perplexity citations within 30–45 days of publication. Brand becomes citable by LLMs for "smart spoon," "AI nutrition tracker," and "automated food logging" queries.

High No sitemap.xml confirmed or submitted to Google Search Console — indexation status unknown Technical SEO ▾

3. Submit sitemap URL in GSC → Sitemaps section.
4. Run URL Inspection on homepage to confirm Google can render it correctly.

Expected Result

All 4 pages confirmed indexed within 1–2 weeks of sitemap submission. GSC begins reporting impressions and click data, providing a baseline for all future SEO measurement. Core Web Vitals data available within 28 days of property connection.

Medium LinkedIn and Instagram links exist in footer but are image-only — Googlebot cannot crawl them, and no sameAs schema connects them to the brand entity Social / PR / Technical ▾
Problem

Spoonified has both a LinkedIn and Instagram account linked in the footer — but Framer renders these as image/SVG icon elements with no visible <a href> anchor text in the crawled HTML. Googlebot sees "Follow us" as plain text with no linked destination. The social accounts exist but are invisible to search engines and AI models that parse the site's link graph.

Opportunity

Properly linked social profiles serve two functions: (1) they pass link equity to the brand's social pages, helping them rank in branded search results; (2) they are the mechanism by which Google and AI models connect the website entity to its social profiles. Without a crawlable link + sameAs schema, Spoonified's LinkedIn and Instagram cannot be cited as confirmed brand properties by AI systems.

How to Fix

1. In Framer, replace image-only social icon components with linked icon components — ensure each has an <a href="https://linkedin.com/company/spoonified"> wrapping the icon (not just an onClick or a CSS background image).
2. Add rel="noopener" and a visible aria-label="Follow Spoonified on LinkedIn" for accessibility.
3. Add both URLs to the sameAs array in the Organization JSON-LD schema on the homepage.

Expected Result

Social profile links crawlable by Googlebot within 1–2 weeks of fix. LinkedIn and Instagram pages begin appearing in branded SERP results (knowledge panel) within 30 days. sameAs schema strengthens brand entity disambiguation for AI models, increasing citation probability within 45–60 days.

Crawlable social links + entity schema in 1 week
